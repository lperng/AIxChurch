Believers & Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Biblical Principles and Practical Action Guidelines

Core Principles for the AI Era

Purpose Principle

Use AI to Glorify God, Not Replace Him

"In their hearts humans plan their course, but the LORD establishes their steps."

Proverbs 16:9

Application: Is AI being used to serve more effectively and spread truth, or as a shortcut to avoid thinking? Let AI be an assistant in our divine mission, not the master that directs our path.

Stewardship Principle

Use AI's Power Wisely, Beware Its Authority

"Now it is required that those who have been given a trust must prove faithful."

1 Corinthians 4:2

Application: We are entrusted to manage God-given creativity, and AI is its product. We should use its capabilities wisely, take responsibility for the ethical issues it brings (like bias, unemployment), and advocate for affected groups.

Discernment Principle

Verify AI's Output, Don't Trust Its Wisdom

"And find out what pleases the Lord."

Ephesians 5:10

Application: AI responses may contain biases, errors, or "hallucinations." For factual, theological, and ethical questions, AI's answers must not be the final authority; we must return to Scripture and reliable spiritual authorities for verification.

Human-centric Principle

Affirm the Unique Value of the "Image of God"

"So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them."

Genesis 1:27

Application: AI can imitate, but it cannot possess the image of God, a soul, a conscience, or true love. Cherish and prioritize the development of uniquely human empathy, creativity, and relational connection, rather than outsourcing them to machines.

Specific Actions for the AI Era

Wise Use

Be the Master of the Tool

Use AI to increase efficiency (e.g., organizing data, drafting documents), but core critical thinking, pastoral care, and final decisions must be made by you.

Wise Use

Protect Personal and Group Privacy

Be highly vigilant about inputting sensitive information (personal testimonies, prayer requests, etc.) when using AI tools to prevent data misuse.

Mission-Oriented

Assist in Ministry and Research

Use AI for biblical background research, language translation, and theological data compilation, dedicating the time saved to ministry that requires more human interaction.

Mission-Oriented

Develop Creative Gospel Ministries

Use AI to generate images, music, and videos to convey the timeless gospel message in a medium that is easily understood and resonates with contemporary audiences.

Education & Dialogue

Promote AI Literacy in the Church

Hold seminars or book clubs to discuss AI ethics, its social impact, and Christian responses from a biblical worldview.

Education & Dialogue

Guide the Next Generation in Discernment

Teach children and youth that their identity and value come from Christ, not their ability to master technology, and help them develop critical thinking habits.
